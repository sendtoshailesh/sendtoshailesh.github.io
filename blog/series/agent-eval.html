AI Agent Evals: Why SWE-bench Isn't Enough Before Production

Shailesh Mishra · July 2025 · ~38 min total reading time

A practitioner's guide to building behavioral evaluation systems for AI agents. From what SWE-bench misses to the Sourdough Test to operational lessons — based on building a real eval system for 8 Copilot agents in Azure/git-ape, with 38 tasks and 3 grader types.

What You'll Learn

  • Why AI agent benchmarks (SWE-bench 74-78%) don't predict production behavior (35-50% PR acceptance)
  • Three silent failure modes and the grader types designed to catch each one
  • The Sourdough Test — one absurd prompt that catches model-wide persona regressions
  • A three-layer grading system (text, tool_constraint, prompt) with $0 deterministic layers
  • Operational lessons: cost profiles ($3-8/run), advisory vs. blocking CI, and the gotcha hall of fame

Reading Sequence

Start with Part 1 for the why — the benchmark gap, the failure taxonomy, and the Sourdough Test. Then Part 2 for the how — the grading system, the regressions it caught, and the 4-week playbook.
